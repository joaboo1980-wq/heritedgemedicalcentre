# Laboratory Sample States - Complete Workflow Guide

## Overview

Every lab sample goes through a series of states from order creation to final result delivery. Here's the complete journey:

---

## 8 Possible Sample States

### 1. 📋 **PENDING** - Order Created, Awaiting Sample
**Status:** `'pending'`

**What it means:**
- Doctor has ordered the lab test
- Sample has NOT been collected yet from the patient
- Sample is waiting to be collected or patient hasn't arrived

**Who sees it:** Lab technician in "Pending" tab

**What happens next:**
- Patient comes to lab
- Technician collects sample
- Status changes to → `sample_collected`

**Related fields:**
- `created_at` - When order was created
- `ordered_by` - Which doctor ordered it
- `priority` - normal or urgent

**Actions available:**
- ✅ **Process** - Mark sample as collected
- ❌ **Reject** - Reject order at this stage (patient no-show, etc.)
- 🗑️ **Cancel** - Cancel the order

---

### 2. 🧪 **SAMPLE_COLLECTED** - Sample Received, Ready for QC
**Status:** `'sample_collected'`

**What it means:**
- Physical sample is in the lab
- Sample has been registered/logged in
- Quality control checks are pending
- Not yet started the actual testing

**Who sees it:** Lab tech in "Samples Rcvd" tab

**Timeline:**
- Patient provided sample
- Lab receives and identifies sample
- QC validation about to happen (checks for hemolysis, sufficient volume, etc.)

**What happens next:**
- Either:
  - ✅ QC passes → Move to → `in_progress` (testing begins)
  - ❌ QC fails → → `rejected` (insufficient sample, contamination, etc.)

**Related fields:**
- `sample_collected_at` - Timestamp when physically collected
- `priority` - Still matters for processing order

**Actions available:**
- 🔧 **Start Testing** - Move to in_progress
- ❌ **Reject** - Reject if QC fails (provides reason)

---

### 3. ⚙️ **IN_PROGRESS** - Testing Underway
**Status:** `'in_progress'`

**What it means:**
- Laboratory equipment is actively analyzing the sample
- Tests are running
- Results not yet available
- Time-sensitive phase (tests may take hours or days depending on test type)

**Who sees it:** Lab tech in "In Progress" tab

**Timeline:**
- Sample passed QC checks
- Tests started (could be biochemistry, hematology, microbiology, etc.)
- Equipment processing sample
- Results being generated

**What happens next:**
- Test completes
- Results generated by analyzer or technician
- Status changes to → `completed`

**Related fields:**
- `turnaround_hours` - Expected time to complete (from lab_tests table)
- `priority` - Urgent tests get priority processing

**Actions available:**
- 🛑 **Cancel** - Stop the test (equipment error, patient request, etc.)
- 📋 **Details** - View test details

**Common duration:** Hours to 1-2 days depending on test type

---

### 4. ✓ **COMPLETED** - Testing Finished, Results Ready
**Status:** `'completed'`

**What it means:**
- Analyzer/technician has finished testing
- Preliminary results exist
- **NOT YET VERIFIED** - Results are not yet approved
- Critical: Results must be reviewed by qualified supervisor before release

**Who sees it:** Lab tech in "Completed" tab

**Timeline:**
- Testing finished
- Analyzer produced results
- Results in system but NOT released to doctor
- Supervisor must review before approval

**What happens next:**
- **ISO 15189 Requirement:** Results must be verified by qualified reviewer
- Supervisor reviews results
- Either:
  - ✅ Results approved → Move to → `verified`
  - ❌ Issues found → Could reject or request retest

**Related fields:**
- `result_value` - The numerical/text result
- `result_notes` - Any technician notes
- `is_abnormal` - Flag if result is outside normal range
- `completed_by` - Which technician/analyzer
- `completed_at` - Timestamp

**This is a CRITICAL step** - Prevents erroneous results from reaching doctors

**Actions available:**
- 🔍 **Mark for Verify** - Route to verification step
- 📋 **Details** - View test result

---

### 5. 🔍 **NEEDS_VERIFY** (Virtual Status) - Results Awaiting Supervisor Review
**Status:** `'completed'` + `verified_at IS NULL`

**What it means:**
- Results exist but not yet reviewed
- Supervisor/lab director must review
- **ISO 15189 REQUIRED STEP**
- Quality assurance gate before results released

**Who sees it:** Lab supervisor/director in "Needs Verify" tab

**Timeline:**
- Results completed
- Waiting in verification queue
- Could be there from minutes to hours/days depending on workload

**What must happen:**
- Lab director/supervisor reviews:
  - Are results logical?
  - Do they match clinical context?
  - Are there instrument errors?
  - Are QC checks passed?
  - Result in appropriate range?

**What happens next:**
- ✅ Supervisor approves → Move to → `verified`
- ❌ Supervisor rejects → Results recalled, may need retest

**Related fields:**
- `verified_by` - Empty until supervisor reviews
- `verified_at` - Empty until approval

**Actions available:**
- ✅ **Verify Results** - Supervisor approves result
- 📋 **Details** - View result for review

**This is the GATEKEEPER** - Makes sure patients get correct results

---

### 6. ✅ **VERIFIED** - Results Approved, Ready for Doctor
**Status:** `'verified'`

**What it means:**
- Results have been reviewed and approved by qualified supervisor
- Supervisor signature/approval is recorded
- Safe to send to doctor
- Doctor can now see and act on results

**Who sees it:** Lab in "Verified" tab

**Timeline:**
- Results analyzed
- Supervisor verified and approved
- Ready for next step (sending to doctor)

**What happens next:**
- Results sent to ordering doctor
- Doctor reviews in their system
- Status can move to → `reported` (when doctor acknowledges)

**Related fields:**
- `verified_by` - UUID of supervisor who approved
- `verified_at` - ISO 8601 timestamp of approval
- `is_abnormal` - Flag if abnormal (doctor notified)

**For abnormal results:**
- Doctor gets alert/notification
- May call patient for follow-up
- Ensures critical results don't get missed

**Actions available:**
- 📤 **Report Results** - Send to doctor/EHR
- 📋 **Details** - View verified result

---

### 7. 📤 **REPORTED** - Results Delivered to Doctor
**Status:** `'reported'`

**What it means:**
- Results have been officially transmitted to ordering doctor
- Doctor's EHR/system has received results
- Results visible to doctor
- Tracking of delivery completion

**Who sees it:** Compliance/audit team

**Timeline:**
- Results verified
- Sent to doctor via EHR interface or message
- Doctor can now review in their patient chart
- No more action needed from lab (unless abnormal)

**This status is for:**
- Audit trail ("results were delivered on X date")
- Compliance with regulations
- Proving results reached doctor
- SLA tracking

**Related fields:**
- `reported_at` - When sent to doctor
- `reported_to_doctor_at` - Explicit timestamp

**No further actions** - Result complete

---

### 8. ❌ **REJECTED** - Sample Invalid or Test Failed
**Status:** `'rejected'`

**What it means:**
- Sample could not be used for testing
- OR results are invalid and must be discarded
- Patient must provide new sample
- Documented reason required (audit trail)

**Who sees it:** Lab in "Rejected" tab

**When rejection happens:**
1. **At sample receipt stage** (sample_collected)
   - Insufficient volume
   - Hemolyzed (blood cells burst)
   - Clotted (should be liquid)
   - Wrong sample type
   - Contaminated
   - Mislabeled

2. **During analysis** (in_progress)
   - Equipment malfunction
   - Analyzer error
   - QC failed on results

3. **At verification** (completed)
   - Results clearly erroneous
   - Doesn't match clinical context
   - Instrument failure detected

**Related fields:**
- `rejection_reason` - **WHY it was rejected** (required)
- `rejected_by` - UUID of who rejected it
- `rejected_at` - Timestamp of rejection

**What happens next:**
- Doctor is notified
- New sample may be ordered
- No result delivered to doctor
- Previous test marked invalid
- Audit trail shows what went wrong

**Example rejection reasons:**
```
- Insufficient sample volume
- Hemolyzed sample
- Clotted sample
- Wrong sample type
- Contaminated sample
- Mislabeled specimen
- Sample integrity compromised
- QC failed
- Equipment malfunction
```

**Compliance importance:**
- Required for ISO 15189 audits
- Tracks sample failure patterns
- Identifies equipment issues
- Prevents patient harm from bad results

**Actions available:**
- View rejection reason
- View who rejected it and when
- View details for analysis

---

### 9. 🗑️ **CANCELLED** - Test Order Cancelled
**Status:** `'cancelled'`

**What it means:**
- Test order was cancelled (not rejected)
- Could be doctor request, patient refusal, etc.
- No testing happened
- No result generated

**When cancellation happens:**
- Before sample collection
- After sample collection but before testing
- During testing if patient requests

**Difference from Rejected:**
- **Rejected:** Sample quality issue (science problem)
- **Cancelled:** Administrative decision (doctor/patient decision)

**Related fields:**
- `cancelled_at` - When order was cancelled
- `cancelled_by` - Who made the decision

**No actions** - Order is closed

## State Transitions Quick Reference

| Current State | ✅ Next States | ❌ Blocked | Duration |
|--------------|------------|---------|----------|
| **PENDING** | sample_collected, cancelled, rejected | — | Hours/Days |
| **SAMPLE_COLLECTED** | in_progress, rejected | completed, verified | Minutes |
| **IN_PROGRESS** | completed, cancelled | pending, sample_collected | Hours/Days |
| **COMPLETED** | verified, rejected | — | Minutes to hours |
| **NEEDS_VERIFY** | verified, rejected | — | Minutes to hours |
| **VERIFIED** | reported | completed, in_progress, pending | Immediate |
| **REPORTED** | — (end state) | — | Final state |
| **REJECTED** | — (end state) | — | Final state |
| **CANCELLED** | — (end state) | — | Final state |

## Real-World Examples

### Example 1: Happy Path (Normal Flow)
```
9:00 AM  PENDING → Doctor orders blood glucose test
9:15 AM  SAMPLE_COLLECTED → Patient arrives, blood drawn
9:20 AM  IN_PROGRESS → Analyzer starts processing
9:25 AM  COMPLETED → Results: 95 mg/dL (normal)
9:30 AM  NEEDS_VERIFY → Lab director reviews
9:31 AM  VERIFIED → Director approves (normal range)
9:35 AM  REPORTED → Results sent to doctor
         ✅ Doctor sees: "Glucose normal"
```

### Example 2: Rejection After QC
```
9:00 AM  PENDING → Order for CBC (complete blood count)
9:15 AM  SAMPLE_COLLECTED → Sample received
9:16 AM  QC Check → Sample is hemolyzed (red cells burst)
9:20 AM  REJECTED → Reason: "Hemolyzed sample"
         🔴 Doctor notified: "Please recollect sample"
         ❌ Patient called back to lab
```

### Example 3: Abnormal Results with Alert
```
9:00 AM  PENDING → Potassium level test
9:15 AM  SAMPLE_COLLECTED
9:25 AM  IN_PROGRESS
9:35 AM  COMPLETED → Results: 6.8 mmol/L (HIGH - abnormal)
9:40 AM  NEEDS_VERIFY → Director reviews (critical high!)
9:41 AM  VERIFIED → Director approves, flags as abnormal
9:42 AM  REPORTED → Results sent to doctor + ALERT
         🔴 Doctor gets priority notification
         ⚠️ Doctor must review immediately (possible medical emergency)
```

### Example 4: Equipment Error
```
9:00 AM  PENDING → Liver function tests
9:15 AM  SAMPLE_COLLECTED
9:25 AM  IN_PROGRESS
10:00 AM Analyzer crashes mid-test
10:15 AM REJECTED → Reason: "Equipment malfunction"
         ❌ Test must be restarted
         📞 Doctor called about delay
```

## Key ISO 15189 Requirements Met

✅ **Sample tracking** - All 8 states tracked
✅ **Quality control** - Sample_collected + QC validation
✅ **Verification** - VERIFIED status + verified_by/verified_at
✅ **Audit trail** - Rejection reason + who/when tracked
✅ **Abnormal alerts** - Flagged in VERIFIED status
✅ **Result validity** - Cannot complete without verification
✅ **Error documentation** - Rejection reasons recorded

## Summary

A lab sample goes through up to 7 stages (plus 2 end states):

1. 📋 **PENDING** - Waiting for patient sample
2. 🧪 **SAMPLE_COLLECTED** - Sample arrived, ready for QC
3. ⚙️ **IN_PROGRESS** - Analyzer testing
4. ✓ **COMPLETED** - Results ready, needs verification
5. 🔍 **NEEDS_VERIFY** - Supervisor review gate
6. ✅ **VERIFIED** - Approved, safe to send
7. 📤 **REPORTED** - Delivered to doctor ✅ SUCCESS

**Or at any point:**
- ❌ **REJECTED** - Sample/results invalid, new sample needed
- 🗑️ **CANCELLED** - Order cancelled

This workflow ensures **patient safety, regulatory compliance (ISO 15189), and complete audit trails** for every single test.
